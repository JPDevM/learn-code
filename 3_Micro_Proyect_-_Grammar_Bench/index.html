    • Present tense verb be [+], subject pronouns: I, you, etc.

    [+] = possesive form

    Full form Contraction
    I am your teacher. I'm your teacher.
    You are in room 7. You're in room 7.
    He is Mike. He's Mike.
    She is Hannah. She's Hannah.
    It is a school. It's a school.
    We are students. We're students.
    You are in Class 2. You're in Class 2.
    They are teachers. They're teachers.
    • Always use a subject pronoun (you, he, etc.) with a verb, e.g.
      It's a school. NOT It's a school.
      They're teachers. NOT Are teachers.
    • Always use capital I, e.g.
      He's Mike and I'm Sally. NOT i'm Salli.
      With other pronouns onli use a capital letter when it's the first word in a sentence.
    • you = singular and plural.
    • Use he for a man, she for a woman, and it for a thing.
    • Use they for people and things.
    Contractions
    • In contractions ' = a missing letter. e.g.
      'm = am.
    • use contractions in conversation and in informal writing. e.g.
      an email to a friend.

    Present tense verb be [-] and [?]

    I'm not American.
    She isn't from London.
    They aren't Spanish.
    Are you Polish? Yes. I am.
    Is she Russian? No. she isn't.

    • In questions, put am, are, is. before I, you, he, etc.
      Are you German? NOT You are German?
      Where are you from? NOT Where you are from?
    • Don't use contractions in positive short answers.
      Are von Russian? Yes, I am. NOT Yes I'm.
